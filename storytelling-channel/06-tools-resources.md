## üìö Research & Story Finding Tools

### Story Sources (Where to Find Content)

#### **Primary Research Sources**:

**1. Wikipedia** (Starting Point Only)
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Use**: Initial overview, get the basics
- **Warning**: NOT a final source, verify everything
- **Tip**: Check the "References" section for credible sources

**2. Britannica** 
- **Cost**: FREE (limited) or $70/year
- **Use**: Credible historical facts
- **Quality**: Encyclopedic, fact-checked
- **Link**: britannica.com

**3. Archive.org** (Wayback Machine + More)
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Use**: 
  - Historical documents
  - Old newspapers (pre-1950s)
  - Out-of-print books
  - Public domain images/videos
- **Gold Mine for**: Historical stories, old photographs
- **Link**: archive.org

**4. Newspapers.com or NewspaperArchive.com**
- **Cost**: $10-20/month
- **Use**: True crime, historical events, firsthand accounts
- **Best for**: Finding original reporting on cases
- **Alternative**: Local library (free newspaper database access)

**5. Academic Databases** (if accessible)
- Google Scholar: FREE
- JSTOR: Often accessible via library
- ResearchGate: FREE
- **Use**: In-depth research, verified facts, expert analysis

#### **Reddit Communities** (Story Ideas):

**Gold Mines**:
- **r/UnresolvedMysteries**: Active community, well-researched posts
- **r/TrueCrime**: Current and historical cases
- **r/AskHistorians**: Expert-verified historical facts
- **r/Paranormal**: Unexplained phenomena (verify carefully)
- **r/MandelaEffect**: Strange collective memories

**How to Use**:
- Browse "Top" posts of all time
- Look for well-sourced stories
- Check comments for additional context
- ALWAYS verify on external sources

#### **YouTube Channels** (Inspiration, NOT Sources):

Watch for story ideas, but verify facts independently:
- **Lemmino**: High-quality mystery documentaries
- **Nexpo**: Internet mysteries
- **Barely Sociable**: Deep dives into unexplained
- **Fascinating Horror**: Disaster stories

**Note**: Never plagiarize. Use for inspiration, create your own narrative.

## ü§ñ AI Tools (Research & Creation)

### **ChatGPT** (You have this!)
- **Cost**: FREE, Plus: $20/month
- **Use for**:
  - Story research assistance
  - Script drafting
  - Hook generation
  - Fact-checking prompts
  - Content repurposing

#### Best Prompts for Storytelling:

**Research Helper**:
```
"I'm researching [topic]. Give me:
1. Key facts and timeline
2. Lesser-known details
3. Conflicting theories or accounts
4. Reliable sources to verify this information"
```

**Script Writer**:
```
"Write a 60-second story script about [topic].

Structure:
- Hook (1-2 sentences, dramatic opener)
- Setup (context in 2-3 sentences)
- Rising action (build tension, 3-4 sentences)
- Twist or climax (dramatic reveal, 2 sentences)
- Resolution (1-2 sentences)
- Engagement hook (question or CTA)

Tone: Mysterious, compelling, factual
Word count: 150-180 words
Target audience: Ages 18-45, mystery enthusiasts"
```

**Hook Generator**:
```
"Generate 10 hooks for a story about [topic].

Each hook should:
- Be 5-10 words maximum
- Create immediate curiosity
- Use specific numbers or details
- Make a bold statement or ask a provocative question

Format examples:
- 'In 1995, she vanished. In 2023, she knocked on her door.'
- '918 people died in one day. Only 1 survived to tell the truth.'
```

**Repurposing**:
```
"Repurpose this Instagram Reel script into:
1. YouTube video title (60 chars max, SEO-optimized)
2. YouTube description (first 150 chars as hook)
3. Twitter thread (5-7 tweets)
4. LinkedIn post (professional angle)

Original script: [paste script]"
```

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### "My voiceovers sound robotic"
**Fix**:
- Use ElevenLabs (best quality)
- Adjust stability to 75-80% (adds variation)
- Add pauses in script with "..." or commas
- Slow down speed to 0.9x

### "I can't find good stock footage"
**Fix**:
- Search MORE specific terms: "dark forest night fog"
- Use Archive.org for historical content
- Layer multiple clips (atmosphere + subject)
- Add effects in Canva (darkness, grain, zoom)

### "Stories aren't getting views"
**Fix**:
- Hook is probably weak (first 1 second)
- Check watch time % (if below 50%, pacing is off)
- Test 3 different hooks for same story
- Study viral storytelling accounts, analyze their hooks

### "Running out of story ideas"
**Fix**:
- Set up Reddit alerts for r/UnresolvedMysteries
- Browse Archive.org newspaper scans
- Ask audience: "What should I cover next?"
- Historical events often have unknown angles

## üí° Tool Hacks

### Canva Hacks:
1. **Save custom color palettes** for each story category
2. **Create folders** by theme (Crime, History, etc.)
3. **Use "Brand Kit"** to store fonts, logos
4. **Duplicate master template** 21 times, customize each
5. **Keyboard shortcuts**: "D" to duplicate, "R" to resize

### ElevenLabs Hacks:
1. **Save favorite voices** for quick access
2. **Use "..." for dramatic pauses**
3. **CAPS for emphasis** in script (AI reads louder)
4. **Download in highest quality**, compress later if needed

### ChatGPT Hacks:
1. **Create "Saved Prompts" doc** in Notion
2. **Batch requests**: "Give me 30 mystery hooks"
3. **Ask for variations**: "Rewrite hook 5 different ways"
4. **Fact-check**: "Verify these facts about [topic]"
